# Roadmap: competitor positioning and launch window

Status: Strategic product and go-to-market note

## Context

Smoke 'Em / Smoke'em Claysports appears to be building a clay sports scorekeeping and performance app with social, squad, stats, coaching and club features.

Based on observed public social media screenshots, the product appears early in market presence with a small Instagram following and limited public footprint. If it is not yet widely available through App Store or Google Play, that creates a potential launch window for Clay Performance Lab.

This should increase urgency, but it should not lead to a rushed low-quality launch.

## Strategic interpretation

The existence of Smoke 'Em validates that there is a market for clay shooting apps beyond simple score entry.

It also shows that several ideas in our roadmap are not unique by themselves:

- shot-by-shot logging
- squad scoring
- season statistics
- station maps
- social feed
- coaching views
- clubs and live events
- free/pro/premium levels

Clay Performance Lab must therefore compete through a sharper position, better local fit, stronger discipline handling and useful analysis.

## Launch-window risk

If another app reaches App Store / Google Play and builds distribution first, it may become harder to win mindshare among early users.

The risk is not only features. It is distribution, habit and community:

- shooters may start logging history in another app
- clubs may adopt another scorekeeping tool
- coaches may become familiar with another reporting format
- social features become more valuable when one app already has the network

## Opportunity for Clay Performance Lab

Clay Performance Lab can still take a strong position if we move quickly in the right order.

Best areas to differentiate:

- Nordic and European discipline support
- Leirduesti, Compak Sporting, English Sporting, Sporttrap, Jegertrap and Nordisk Trap
- Leirdue.net import
- scorecard photo import with review before save
- shared competition setups and post/target definitions
- discipline-specific Performance Reports
- station/plate/stand analysis with correct rule mapping
- training versus competition separation
- coach-ready exports without requiring social-feed adoption

## What not to copy blindly

Do not chase every competitor feature immediately.

Deprioritize for now:

- broad social feed
- public trash-talk/comment feed
- trophies as a main product pillar
- full club event management
- paid club tier before core user value is stable
- leaderboards before data quality and discipline mapping are trustworthy

These can be valuable later, but they should not distract from the main launch value.

## Recommended next-phase focus

The next product phase should aim for a credible first public release, not a perfect full platform.

Priority order:

1. Stabilize existing core flows.
2. Polish visual design and first impression.
3. Improve dashboard and Performance Report so the app feels useful immediately.
4. Deliver quick tester feedback improvements.
5. Strengthen scorecard/photo import and review flows.
6. Add discipline-specific visuals for trap, skeet and sporting where data supports it.
7. Expand discipline support through shared engines and versioned rule sets.
8. Prepare App Store / Google Play launch materials.

## Launch quality bar

Speed matters, but the app should not be launched publicly if it creates distrust.

Minimum launch quality should include:

- stable login and onboarding
- clear dashboard
- reliable manual result entry
- reliable current imports where supported
- no known data-loss issues
- clear training/competition separation
- clean mobile UI
- honest feature descriptions
- no overpromising on AI or analysis
- basic support/contact route

## Go-to-market channels

Relevant early channels:

- Norwegian and Nordic clay shooting communities
- selected beta shooters
- coaches and high-level shooters
- clubs with active training groups
- Instagram/Facebook groups
- competition weekends where users can test the app directly
- App Store / Google Play once launch quality is met

## Guardrails

- Do not let competitor pressure create sloppy architecture.
- Do not launch broad claims before data and discipline logic are correct.
- Do not copy visual identity or wording from competitors.
- Use competitor observations to sharpen our own positioning, not to duplicate their product.
- Treat public competitor data as directional until verified.

## Locked decisions

1. Smoke 'Em should be tracked as a real competitor/reference, especially around live scoring, social, squad, stats and coaching.
2. Clay Performance Lab should move faster toward public channels, but not at the cost of trust or data quality.
3. The first public launch should emphasize the areas where Clay Performance Lab can be strongest: Nordic/European disciplines, imports, Performance Report, discipline-specific analysis and coach-ready sharing.
4. Social feed, trophies, leaderboards and club premium are later opportunities, not immediate launch blockers.
